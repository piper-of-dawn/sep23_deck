Classification of Financial Instruments

  • Ownership

    Does the instrument holder has an ownership in the company?

  • Maturity

    Time period before the instrument is converted into cash.

  • Interest rate

    Is it fixed or floating (variable)?

  • Seniority

    Who gets paid first?

  • Security

    Is the instrument backed by a security?

  • Convertibility

    Is the instrument convertible into another instrument?

Types of Equity Finance Instruments

Equity Finance Instruments for a private (unlisted) company

Venture Capital

Venture capital is a form of private equity and a type of financing that investors provide to startup companies and small businesses that are believed to have long-term growth potential. However, it does not always take a monetary form; it can also be provided in the form of technical or managerial expertise.

Angel Investor

Angel investor is usually an individual with a very high net-worth who invest in the business in return for ownership stake. Angel investor is different from Venture Capitalist in the sense that venture capital pools the funds of others to invest in a company.

Indian firms funded by private equity

  • Over 74% stake in Paytm is held by Alibaba Group, Softbank Vision Fund etc.

  • Funders of Flipkart include E-bay, Microsoft and Tencent

  • Ola is majorly funded by Tencent and Softbank Group

Equity Finance Instruments for a publically listed company

Equity shares

Equity shares are also known as ordinary shares. They are the form of fractional or part ownership in which the shareholder. Investors in such shares hold the right to vote, share profits and claim assets of a company. In case company closes, they are the last claimants over the assets of the company.

Preference shares

Unlike equity shares where dividends depends upon the profits of the company, preference shareholders are paid fixed rate of dividend every year irrespective of the profits of the company.

Shares with differential voting rights

Ownership is seperated from management. Owners of such shares may have limited or no voting rights.

Types of Debt Finance Instruments

Debt Finance Instruments

Debentures

A debenture is a type of bond or other debt instrument that is unsecured by collateral. Since debentures have no collateral backing, debentures must rely on the creditworthiness and reputation of the issuer for support.

Corporate Bonds

Bonds on the other hands are issued generally by the government, central bank or large companies are backed by a security. Bonds also ensure payment of fixed interest rates to the lenders of the money. On maturity of the bond, the principal amount is paid back. Bonds essentially work the way loans do.
